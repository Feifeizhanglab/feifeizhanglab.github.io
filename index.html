Engineering the tumor microenvironment for cancer immunotherapy

The Feifei Zhang Lab develops CRISPR, gene delivery, and spatial-omics approaches to reprogram immune ecosystems in cancer and to build next-generation immunotherapies.

About the Lab

We combine functional genomics, tumor immunology, and translational engineering.

Our lab studies how tumor cells and stromal and immune compartments shape the tumor microenvironment, and how these interactions can be rewired to improve cancer immunotherapy. We develop CRISPR-based screening platforms, gene delivery systems, and spatial single-cell approaches to identify actionable mechanisms and therapeutic opportunities.

Research Areas

Three current directions that define the lab’s scientific focus.

CRISPR Functional Genomics

We use CRISPR perturbation systems to identify genes and pathways that regulate immune resistance, tumor plasticity, and therapeutic response.

TME Reprogramming

We engineer strategies to convert immunosuppressive tumor microenvironments into immune-active states that support durable anti-tumor responses.

Spatial and Single-Cell Technologies

We integrate spatial transcriptomics and single-cell multi-omics to map cell-state transitions, immune niches, and therapy-induced remodeling.
